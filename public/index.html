What are the Muqatta'at — the 1,400-year mystery
The most studied unsolved mystery in Islamic textual scholarship
29 of the 114 Surahs of the Quran open with sequences of disconnected Arabic letters called Muqatta'at (مقطعات) — meaning "disjoined letters" or "abbreviated letters." They appear immediately after the Bismillah and before the Surah's text begins. Islamic scholarship has never definitively explained their meaning. The earliest companions of Muhammad, the greatest classical scholars — Ibn Abbas, Al-Tabari, Ibn Kathir — all classified them as "mutashabihat" — matters whose meaning is known only to Allah. The decipherment method — Proto-Sinaitic pictographic reading

Arabic and Hebrew both descend from Proto-Sinaitic script (~1800 BC) — the world's first alphabetic writing system, found in the turquoise mines of Sinai. The Hebrew tradition preserved more of the pictographic meanings because the aleph-bet remained more stable.
